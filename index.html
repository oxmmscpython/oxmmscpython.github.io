This is the online textbook for the Oxford course on Python in Scientific Computing, offered to MSc students on the MSc in Mathematical Modelling and Scientific Computing, and to DPhil students. The lecturer for this course is Patrick E. Farrell. Please address all questions about the course to Dr Farrell.

This course is a fork of the MATH50009 Principles of Programming course prepared by David A. Ham, offered to mathematics undergraduates at Imperial College London. Dr Farrell is very grateful to Dr Ham for his kind and generous assistance in this matter. The videos recorded by Dr Ham sometimes refer to Imperial-specific matters such as assessments; please ignore these.

The Imperial term is longer than the Oxford one (which is only 8 weeks long). Oxford students should therefore read the chapters and do the exercises in the following weeks:
